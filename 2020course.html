The ultimate 2020 cv Writing guide

Remember cv is not for getting the job it is the first step or to get you to the interview. Before you start making your cv be sure to read the following guide or list of thing you should consider before writing cv.

  • Be consistent with your CV layout
  • Make your CV brief and relevant Don’t be one of those candidates stuck in the nineties who think they have to include every single detail about their lives on their CVs.
  • List Your Relevant Work Experience & Key Achievements
  • Contact info at the top Full name, Professional title, Email address, Telephone number, LinkedIn profile, Home address
  • Start with a CV Personal Profile. that shows
    • Who are you?
    • What can you offer the company?
    • What are your career goals?
  • Education Section don’t add your middle and high school in the section unless you are told to do because it is not important
  • Sample CV Additional Sections Industry awards, Professional certifications, Publications, Professional affiliations, Conferences attended, Additional training
  • References: Like including an address on your CV, adding your referees to the end of your CV is no longer standardized. You can include a line that reads ‘references available on request’, but if you don’t have room, it’s acceptable to remove it altogether.
  • Length it is better to have one page cv but if not 2 is maximum .
  • Font make your font easy to read and make them around 12px to 14 and 16 to 20 for the heading and use easy fonts like areal

    Do not include the following things in to your cv

  • Age and date of birth: The only dates that should be on your CV are from employment and your qualifications. Your age doesn’t affect your ability to do the job, and it’s illegal for employers to ask about age under the Equality Act 2010.
  • Marital status: Like your age, your marital status and dependents don’t affect your ability to do your job. These details are protected characteristics under the Equality Act 2010, and it’s against the law for employers to ask about them, so don’t include them on your CV.
  • Get photos off of your CV remember cv is just to get you to the next level.
